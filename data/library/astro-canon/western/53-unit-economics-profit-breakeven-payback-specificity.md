# Western Unit Economics, Profit-Margin, Break-Even, And Payback Specificity Pack

Use this pack only when the user asks about profit quality, margin, unit economics, break-even, payback, LTV/CAC, contribution margin, gross/net profit, cost per order, cost per customer, ARPU/AOV, margin floor, or whether a business model is economically worth doing.

Do not treat unit economics as the same thing as cashflow/runway, price strategy, windfall/speculation, marketing campaign optimization, retention/churn rescue, sales-team QA, team compensation, partner-risk, or technology build. If the user asks "cash left / reserve / runway / burn rate", use the cashflow pack. If the user asks "raise price / discount / coupon / upsell", use the pricing pack. If the user asks "CAC/ROAS for ad creative or funnel traffic", use the marketing pack. This pack is for whether each sale, cohort, customer, product, or plan is profitable after real costs.

## Canon Routing

Western profit judgment must not stop at the 2nd house. Judge the native's profit model by the 2nd/Fortune, MC/10th, 4th/IC, 3rd/Mercury, 7th, 6th, and 8th/12th, then weigh dignity, reception, sect, aspect condition, and timing.

- Asc/chart-ruler or no-time substitute: whether the owner can enforce margin discipline and avoid vanity revenue.
- 2nd/Fortune: revenue per sale, retained money, collection quality, and real margin.
- MC/10th: business model, authority, delivery machine, monetization structure, and profit engine.
- 4th/IC: fixed-cost base, retained assets, inventory/office/platform base, and break-even absorption.
- 3rd/Mercury/7th: sales terms, invoices, buyer promise, customer agreement, channel contract, and pricing communication.
- 6th: labor, service burden, contractors, tools, AI, SaaS, support, fulfillment, and variable cost per unit.
- 8th/12th/Saturn-Mars: debt, tax, refund drag, hidden labor, bad debt, leakage, rework, disputes, and margin killers.
- Transits/returns/profections only when packet-supplied: timing for tightening, pausing, testing, or scaling.

## Answer Contract

For unit economics questions, answer with:

1. one Asc/chart-ruler or no-time substitute margin-discipline line;
2. one 2nd/Fortune revenue-per-unit and collection-quality line;
3. one MC/10th profit-model or operating-engine line;
4. one 4th/IC fixed-cost/break-even-base line;
5. one 3rd/Mercury/7th price-term/invoice/channel-promise line;
6. one 6th variable-cost/staff/tool/vendor line;
7. one 8th/12th/Saturn-Mars hidden-cost/refund/tax/rework-risk line;
8. one timing line from packet-supplied transits/returns/profections when available;
9. one practical verdict: raise margin floor, stop an unprofitable SKU, repackage, cap variable cost, collect before delivery, or test smaller.

Do not translate "high revenue" into "high profit" unless the 6th, 8th, and 12th are handled. If the chart shows expense pressure, say exactly which cost category must be capped before scaling.
