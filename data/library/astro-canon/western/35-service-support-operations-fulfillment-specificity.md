# Western Service, Support, Operations, And Fulfillment Specificity Pack

> Runtime derived summary for Fusion Western. It condenses classical, medieval, early-modern, public-domain modern bridge, and licensed-internal operational rules. Do not quote, cite, reproduce, or expose raw source text.

## Purpose

Use this pack for customer support, service quality, complaint handling, ticket backlog, delivery, fulfillment, onboarding, SLA, operations workload, staff capacity, service recovery, product delivery, and "can we serve customers well" questions.

Do not answer from sales, marketing, price, or reputation alone. Split demand, service promise, staff capacity, customer pressure, communication, operating cost, bottleneck, and timing.

## Core Chain

1. **Capacity and owner**: Ascendant/chart ruler when birth time is known; otherwise Sun/Moon/Mercury/Saturn condition and supplied aspects.
2. **Support message and ticket flow**: 3rd house/Mercury show reply speed, documentation, scripts, ticket clarity, escalation wording, and onboarding instructions.
3. **Customer pressure**: 7th house/ruler shows customer, client, subscriber, platform counterparty, complaint source, and whether recovery is accepted.
4. **Service promise and standard**: MC/10th shows official promise, quality standard, process discipline, and whether the public claim can be fulfilled.
5. **Staff and workload**: 6th house shows employees, queues, repetitive work, support burden, service defects, and operational friction.
6. **Audience/community helpers**: 11th shows support community, warm users, referrals, and whether users help or amplify complaints.
7. **Cost, reserve, and liability**: 2nd/Fortune show support budget and recovery; 8th shows refunds, charge liability, third-party cost; 12th shows hidden loss and burnout.
8. **Timing**: supplied transits/return cycles activate the natal promise; do not invent exact dates.

## Service-Chain Rule

A sale is not a delivered result. Require a chain:

- 3rd/Mercury = support wording, ticket flow, documentation,
- 7th = customer expectation and complaint pressure,
- MC/10th = service promise and SOP,
- 6th = staff, queue, defects, workload,
- 2nd/Fortune/8th = support cost, reserve, liability,
- 12th = hidden leak, burnout, unseen backlog.

If 7th is pressured while 3rd/Mercury is weak, customers complain because communication and recovery are unclear. If MC/10th is strong but 6th/12th are weak, the promise is good but operations cannot carry the load. If 2nd/Fortune/8th are weak, do not expand service before fixing delivery cost and refund exposure.

## No-Time Rule

If birth time is missing, close houses, angles, lots, and house-ruler claims unless packet supplies a reliable fallback. Use Sun/Moon/Mercury/Saturn/Mars condition, aspects, and timing only; avoid confident staffing or SLA claims that depend on houses.
