# Western Hiring, Delegation, And Org Design Specificity Pack

Derived runtime rules for owner-side hiring, delegation, and organization design. Use as private internal guidance only; do not quote or expose source text.

## Scope Gate

Do not treat hiring/delegation as the same thing as applying for a job, generic people networking, customer support backlog, partnership program, marketing traffic, or tech automation. This pack is for building the operator's team: who to hire, what role to delegate, how to structure reporting, when to use contractor/VA/manager, and when to delay/fire.

Trigger examples: hire employee, contractor, freelancer, VA, assistant, ops manager, sales role, support role, specialist, manager; write job description; interview candidates; org chart; team structure; delegation; outsource; paid trial; promote internal lead; replace bad hire; fire with documentation.

## Evidence Chain

1. Asc/chart ruler or no-time substitute: owner load, delegation capacity, personal bottleneck, boundary enforcement, and whether the owner can manage people.
2. MC/10th/Sun/Saturn: role standard, management authority, org chart, accountability, professional function, and whether the hire should be operations, sales, support, or specialist.
3. 3rd/Mercury/9th: job description, interview language, training docs, SOPs, policy, and candidate screening questions.
4. 6th: staff, employee, contractor, VA, service labor, day-to-day workload, and whether tasks can be delegated cleanly.
5. 7th: working agreement, contractor terms, employment expectation, manager relationship, and accountability promise.
6. 11th: team, network, referral hiring, community leverage, and whether a manager layer or sales/support pod can function.
7. 2nd/Fortune/4th/8th: payroll budget, retained systems/assets, margin after labor, cash buffer, and liability exposure.
8. Moon/Venus/Jupiter: culture, morale, loyalty, trust, and whether the team can stay emotionally stable.
9. 6th/8th/12th/Mars-Saturn: bad hire, hidden workload, conflict, burnout, churn, liability, and firing/documentation risk.
10. Timing: transits/returns/refDate/timing fields must support hire, trial, promotion, restructure, replacement, or delay.

## No-Time Rule

If birth time is missing, do not pretend houses/MC are exact. Use Moon, Sun, visible planets, sect proxy when available, timed transits by date, and any packet-provided no-time substitute. Say clearly that house-specific hiring calls are lower confidence.

## Decision Vocabulary

Final call must choose concrete hiring action, not vague team advice: do not hire yet / hire contractor / hire VA / hire assistant / hire ops manager / hire sales role / hire support role / hire specialist / outsource / fractional manager / build SOP first / write job description / run paid trial / promote internal lead / reduce team / replace bad hire / fire with documentation.

## Answer Contract

Every hiring/delegation answer must include:

- one Asc/chart-ruler or no-time substitute owner-load/delegation-capacity line
- one MC/10th role-standard/management-authority line
- one 3rd/Mercury/9th JD/interview/SOP line
- one 6th staff/team/contractor line
- one 7th working-agreement/expectation line
- one 2nd/Fortune/4th/8th payroll-ROI/assets line
- one Moon/Venus/Jupiter culture/loyalty/morale line
- one 6th/8th/12th bad-hire/conflict/liability line
- one timing line
- one concrete action line using the decision vocabulary, e.g. hire ops manager only if authority, payroll, staff, and timing evidence support it.
