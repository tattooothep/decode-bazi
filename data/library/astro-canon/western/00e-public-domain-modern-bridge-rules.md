## Modern Natal Synthesis Bridge

- A chart should be read as a working whole: luminaries, Asc/chart ruler, house rulers, dominant planets, aspect structure, and repeated topics.
- Repetition is decisive: one weak signal is a note; three signals on the same topic become a judgment.
- Planet monographs and notable-nativity witnesses may enrich wording only after the packet shows the planet is relevant by rulership, angularity, dignity, tight aspect, dominant-planet ranking, or topic rulership.
- Degree/face or periodical material may refine a planet or angle only when that exact planet/angle/degree/topic is central to the question; it must not replace house ruler, dignity, aspect, sect, lot, or timing testimony.
- Morin-style determination is the anti-generic default: if a planet rules one house and sits in another, name the two life areas and the direction of causation instead of giving a broad sign description.
- A sign describes style; a house describes field of life; a planet describes function; an aspect describes interaction; dignity describes operating quality.
- Do not lead with sign keywords if house rulers and aspects give a more specific story.
- A planet prominent by angle, rulership, tight aspect, or pattern focus deserves more weight than a planet only present by sign.
- When the answer feels broad, force it through the topic matrix: “which house/ruler/aspect makes this true for this person?”

## Aspect Synthesis Bridge

Use aspect meaning in three layers:

1. **Psychological function**: what two drives are trying to coordinate.
2. **Life-field expression**: where this appears by houses and topic lords.
3. **Behavioral pattern**: how the native usually acts when the aspect is triggered.

Aspect strength order:

- tight orb to luminary/angle/chart ruler,
- applying major aspect,
- aspect involving topic ruler of the question,
- repeated by pattern or midpoint/fixed-star evidence,
- minor aspect only if tight or repeated.

Hard aspects:

- Square: friction, training, crisis-to-skill, effort. In strong charts, produces mastery; in weak charts, repeated blockage.
- Opposition: polarity and projection. Often shows up through other people, partners, competitors, or public confrontation.
- Quincunx/inconjunct: adjustment, awkward fit, health/routine strain, mismatch between houses. Use as secondary unless exact.

Soft aspects:

- Trine: ease and natural fluency. Can be talent or complacency depending on planet condition.
- Sextile: opportunity through action, learning, contact, and choice. More active than passive when connected to Mercury/Mars/3rd/11th.

Conjunction:

- Fusion and amplification. Judge by planetary nature, dignity, house, and whether benefic/malefic condition dominates.

## Aspect Pattern Bridge

- **Stellium**: one sign/house/topic dominates attention. Interpret the dispositor and house ruler carefully.
- **T-square**: pressure system with focal/apex planet. The apex planet shows where life demands skill under stress.
- **Grand Trine**: talent circuit. Needs activation if the chart lacks cardinal/Mars/Saturn drive.
- **Grand Cross**: multiple competing demands; requires structure and prioritization.
- **Yod**: special adjustment or awkward destiny-like pressure. Do not overstate; name the apex planet and houses.
- **Kite**: talent given an outlet through opposition/focus point.
- **Unaspected planet**: function may operate in bursts, extremes, or feel unintegrated. Use only if packet computes unaspected status.

## Timing Bridge

Use only computed packet data.

- Transits describe current external pressure/opportunity.
- Progressions/directions describe inner timing and maturation only if the packet supplies them; Alan Leo's `The Progressed Horoscope` is source support for this layer, not permission for the AI to calculate it from scratch.
- Return cycles show phase: Jupiter for growth/meaning cycle; Saturn for structure/accountability cycle.
- A timing claim should name the trigger: transiting planet, natal target, aspect type, orb, and topic house.
- No exact date should be invented. If exact transit windows are absent, give the ref-date signal and broader practical meaning.
- For user questions about a future year, use the packet’s target-year refDate if present. Do not say “only current year” when refDate has been resolved to the asked year.

## Fixed-Star Bridge

Use fixed stars as amplifiers and descriptors, not standalone fate.

- Prioritize hits to Asc, MC, Sun, Moon, chart ruler, Fortune, 10th ruler, 7th ruler, and topic ruler.
- Conjunction/parallel is stronger than opposition/contraparallel unless the packet prioritizes otherwise.
- Fixed-star nature should modify the planet it touches; do not read the star without the planet/house.
- A benefic star on a weak planet can add visibility/help but does not erase weak dignity.
- A harsh star on a strong planet can show high-stakes ambition or danger of excess, not automatic disaster.
- For user output, translate star symbolism into concrete life channels: reputation, risk, volatility, eloquence, protection, ambition, conflict, success after strain.

## Directions / Progressions Guard

The downloaded sources include timing doctrines, but the current packet only supports ref-date transits and coarse Jupiter/Saturn return cycles unless additional fields are supplied.

Therefore:

- Do not create primary directions.
- Do not create secondary progressions.
- Do not create solar arcs.
- Do not create progressed synastry.
- Do not create return charts.
- If user asks for timing beyond the packet, answer from available transits/return cycles and say the precise timing layer must be computed first.
- If a future-year packet includes only transits/return cycles, give a clear transit-based verdict and state that a progressed-horoscope judgment needs computed progressed positions.

## Practical Answer Shape

For each user question:

1. Start with one decisive statement.
2. Give 5 chart-specific anchors.
3. For each anchor, state the life consequence.
4. If timing is asked, separate “current/future trigger” from “natal baseline”.
5. End with practical actions that match the chart, not generic advice.

Example pattern, not content to copy:

- “Because [planet/ruler] is in [house/sign] and makes [aspect] to [planet/ruler], the career issue is not just ambition; it is a conflict between [topic A] and [topic B].”
- “The support comes from [benefic/soft aspect/reception]; the leakage comes from [malefic/hard aspect/weak dignity].”
- “For relationship, A activates B’s [house] through [planet], while B presses A’s [planet/angle] through [aspect].”
