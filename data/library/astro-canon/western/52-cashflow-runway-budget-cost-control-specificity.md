## Core Chain

1. **Ascendant/chart ruler or no-time substitute**: owner discipline, stress tolerance, and ability to enforce budget decisions.
2. **2nd house/ruler and Part of Fortune**: cash on hand, monthly receipts, billing reliability, and retained money.
3. **4th house/IC**: reserve base, fixed commitments, office/property/infrastructure, and what remains after operations.
4. **MC/10th and Saturn**: operating model, management authority, policy, controls, and whether cuts can be enforced.
5. **3rd/Mercury and 9th**: invoices, accounting reports, payment instructions, tax/compliance documents, and collection communication.
6. **7th house/ruler**: customers, vendors, platforms, counterparties, accounts receivable, payment terms, and delayed payment.
7. **6th house/ruler**: staff, contractors, tools, systems, operations workload, payroll, AI/SaaS/ad cost, and daily burn.
8. **8th/12th houses and hard Saturn/Mars contacts**: debt service, tax, refunds, chargebacks, hidden loss, emergency cost, and margin leak.
9. **11th house**: recurring gains, subscriber base, collection pool, and whether growth supports cashflow.
10. **Timing**: supplied transits/return cycles activate the natal promise; do not invent exact dates.

## Runway Rule

Runway requires both cash and retention:

- 2nd/Fortune = money received and cash on hand,
- 4th = reserve/fixed base,
- MC/10th = operating discipline,
- 3rd/Mercury/9th = invoice/accounting/tax documentation,
- 7th = customer/vendor payment terms,
- 6th = staff/tool/platform burn,
- 8th/12th = debt, refund, tax, hidden loss,
- 11th = recurring gains.

If 2nd is supported but 4th is weak, revenue arrives but reserve does not build. If 4th is supported but 2nd is weak, protect base and collect receivables before expanding. If 6th/8th/12th dominate, operating burn must be capped before scaling.

## No-Time Rule

If birth time is missing, close houses, angles, lots, and house-ruler claims unless packet supplies a reliable fallback. Use Sun/Moon/Mercury/Venus/Jupiter/Saturn condition, aspects, and timing only; avoid confident runway length claims that depend on houses.

## Cost-Cut Rule

Name the cost lane:

- **staff/tool burn**: 6th pressure,
- **vendor/customer term leak**: 7th pressure,
- **invoice/report/tax confusion**: 3rd/Mercury/9th pressure,
- **fixed commitments/infrastructure**: 4th pressure,
- **debt/refund/tax/emergency**: 8th/12th pressure,
- **growth promise without retained cash**: 10th/11th stronger than 2nd/4th.

Good cash evidence allows controlled reinvestment. Strong leak evidence requires freeze, cap, collect, renegotiate, or reserve.
