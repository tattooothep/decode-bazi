# Western Partner Due Diligence, Conflict, And Termination Specificity Pack

Derived runtime rules for business partner vetting, counterparty risk, joint-venture terms, contract breach, buyout, exit, and termination. Use as private internal guidance only; do not quote or expose source text.

## Scope Gate

Do not treat partner due diligence as the same thing as affiliate/referral program design, romantic relationship, generic networking, generic marketing, or generic hiring. This pack is for deciding whether to trust a specific business partner/counterparty, how to structure the deal, how to handle partner conflict, and whether to pause, renegotiate, buy out, terminate, or litigate.

Trigger examples: due diligence, vet partner, background check, partner risk, business partner conflict, cofounder conflict, joint venture dispute, equity split, revenue split, MOU, operating agreement, shareholder agreement, breach, default, buyout, exit clause, terminate partnership, dissolve partnership, non-compete, NDA, confidentiality, partner fraud, partner stole clients, partner not delivering.

## Evidence Chain

1. Asc/chart ruler or no-time substitute: operator judgment, trust boundary, enforcement capacity, and whether the operator gives away control too quickly.
2. 7th/Venus: specific partner/counterparty, contract tie, loyalty, one-to-one obligation, exclusivity, breach, and open opponent risk.
3. MC/10th/Sun/Saturn: decision rights, authority, role ownership, public accountability, operating control, and standards.
4. 2nd/Fortune/8th: equity, revenue split, shared money, debt, capital contribution, payout rights, and financial exposure.
5. 4th: retained assets, IP base, data/customer list ownership, property of the venture, and what remains after exit.
6. 3rd/Mercury/9th: MOU, agreement, NDA, written clause, negotiation, message trail, legal principle, and evidence.
7. 6th/11th: delivery dependency, partner's team/channel/community, execution capacity, staff handoff, and service promises.
8. 8th/12th/Mars-Saturn: fraud, hidden liability, client theft, IP leak, non-performance, lawsuit, penalty, reputation damage, and forced separation.
9. Timing: transits/returns/refDate/timing fields must support signing, renegotiation, audit, pause, buyout, termination, or legal escalation.

## No-Time Rule

If birth time is missing, do not pretend houses/MC are exact. Use Moon, Sun, visible planets, timed transits by date, and packet-provided no-time substitute. Say clearly that partner-house and authority-house calls are lower confidence.

## Decision Vocabulary

Final call must choose a concrete partner-risk action: proceed with strict clauses / sign small pilot only / request documents first / audit money trail / split roles more clearly / renegotiate equity / move to revenue share / pause partnership / issue written warning / buy out / terminate with documentation / avoid partner / escalate to lawyer.

## Answer Contract

Every partner due-diligence/conflict answer must include:

- one Asc/chart-ruler or no-time substitute trust-boundary/enforcement-capacity line
- one 7th/Venus partner/counterparty-contract line
- one MC/10th decision-rights/role-authority line
- one 2nd/Fortune/8th equity-revenue-asset-control line
- one 3rd/Mercury/9th written-clause/evidence/negotiation line
- one 6th/11th delivery-dependency/team-channel line
- one 4th/11th trust-goodwill-retained-asset line
- one 8th/12th fraud/breach/liability/exit-risk line
- one timing line
- one concrete action line using the decision vocabulary.
