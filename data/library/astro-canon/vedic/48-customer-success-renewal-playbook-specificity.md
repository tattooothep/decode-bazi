## Success Chain

1. **Lagna/Moon or no-time substitute**: whether the owner can carry accountability, enforce rhythm, follow up, and keep clients progressing without over-rescuing.
2. **10th/D10/Sun/Saturn**: success standard, service authority, outcome definition, professional milestone, and what "done well" means.
3. **3rd/Mercury/2nd/9th**: check-in messages, progress review questions, reminder cadence, instructions, education content, documentation, and success notes.
4. **7th/Venus**: direct client relationship, renewal conversation, expectations, trust, objections, and willingness to continue.
5. **11th**: cohort/community/customer pool, team handoff, customer-success staff, group momentum, and whether success can be managed at scale.
6. **2nd/4th/8th/11th**: renewal revenue, retained value, expansion account, implementation asset, saved money/time, and whether success turns into durable value.
7. **Moon/5th/9th/Jupiter**: client satisfaction, emotional relief, trust, loyalty, values alignment, and when to ask for referral or testimonial.
8. **6th/8th/12th/Saturn-Mars-Rahu-Ketu**: at-risk clients, non-adoption, missing work, hidden dissatisfaction, over-service, refund pressure, bad-fit rescue, and when to stop chasing.
9. **Dasha/gocara/ashtakavarga**: when to start check-ins, run success reviews, renew, expand, ask for referrals, rescue, downgrade, or close the account.

## No-Time Rule

If birth time is missing, close Lagna/bhava/D10 claims unless the packet supplies reliable fallback. Use Moon, Sun, visible graha condition, dignity, nakshatra only if stable, and broad timing; frame customer-success/renewal advice as lower precision.

## Playbook Mapping

Map from packet evidence, not generic graha keywords:

- **10th/D10/Sun/Saturn strong**: define measurable success milestones and formal review points.
- **3rd/Mercury/2nd/9th strong**: use written check-ins, scorecards, progress notes, education sequence, and reminder templates.
- **7th/Venus strong**: use direct renewal calls, expectation reset, account plan, and relationship-led expansion.
- **11th strong**: use cohort momentum, group office hours, staff triage, community check-ins, and scalable health scoring.
- **2nd/4th/8th/11th strong**: renew around retained value, implementation assets, cost saved, revenue created, or account expansion.
- **Moon/5th/9th/Jupiter strong**: ask for referral/testimonial after relief or trust is visibly created.
- **6th/8th/12th heavy**: run rescue plan, reduce scope, require client action, downgrade, or stop before over-service becomes loss.

## Verdict Rules

- If customer value is strong but communication lane is weak, create success scorecard before asking for renewal.
- If relationship lane is strong but risk lane is active, renew only after expectation reset and visible milestone.
- If scale lane is strong, move success management into group cadence plus exception handling.
- If retained-value lane is strong, ask for expansion after asset/result is delivered, not before.
- If hidden-risk lane repeats, do not over-rescue; set action deadline, downgrade, or close the account.
