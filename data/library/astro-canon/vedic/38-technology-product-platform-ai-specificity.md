# Jyotish Technology, Product, Platform, AI, And Software Specificity Pack

> Runtime derived summary for Fusion Vedic/Jyotish. It condenses Parashari/Jataka, dasha, varga, ashtakavarga, gochara, and licensed-internal operational rules. Do not quote, cite, reproduce, expose OCR/source text, page order, or private paths.

## Purpose

Use this pack for app, website, SaaS, software, AI product, LLM/chatbot, API, database, server, cloud, deploy, automation, integration, security, privacy, data asset, product roadmap, MVP, beta launch, feature decision, and platform reliability questions.

Do not answer from business luck, marketing demand, or service quality alone. Split product idea, specification, engineering capacity, user/platform acceptance, infrastructure, data/security risk, budget, and timing.

## Core Chain

1. **Capacity and product owner**: Lagna/Lagnesha and Moon show ability to carry technical complexity, repeated debugging, and long maintenance. If birth time is missing, use Moon and graha condition only.
2. **Specification, code, and API**: 3rd bhava/Mercury and 2nd speech show product spec, code logic, prompt/API wording, documentation, integration contract, bug report clarity, and developer communication.
3. **Product direction and release standard**: 10th/D10 show roadmap discipline, official product promise, launch standard, and whether the technical claim is credible.
4. **Engineering work and defects**: 6th bhava/lord shows repeated work, bug queue, support workload, QA, technical debt, disputes, and defect fixing.
5. **Users, platform, and integrations**: 7th bhava/lord shows customers, app stores, marketplaces, API counterparties, vendors, and integration acceptance.
6. **Network and adoption**: 11th shows user network, developer community, ecosystem, gains, referrals, and adoption loops.
7. **Infrastructure and data asset**: 4th and 12th show backend base, database, hosting, hidden systems, repository, data store, and invisible maintenance burden.
8. **Budget and liability**: 2nd/11th show runway and cost recovery; 8th shows data/security liability, third-party dependencies, and outside cost exposure. Rahu/Ketu can show AI, cloud, automation, and unusual technical risk when supplied.
9. **Timing**: dasha/gocara/ashtakavarga activation decides whether to build MVP, refactor, launch beta, harden security, scale infrastructure, or wait.

## Product-Chain Rule

A feature is not a stable product. Require a chain:

- 3rd/Mercury/2nd = specification, code, API, prompt, documentation,
- 10th/D10 = product direction and release standard,
- 6th = bugs, QA, repeated engineering work,
- 7th/11th = users, platform, integrations, adoption,
- 4th/12th = infrastructure, backend, data asset, hidden maintenance,
- 2nd/11th/8th = build cost, runway, security/privacy liability.

If 3rd/Mercury is strong but 4th/12th are weak, the idea is clear but the system is unstable. If 10th/D10 is strong but 6th is weak, roadmap is good but engineering execution is thin. If 7th/11th are strong but 2nd/8th are weak, users want it but infrastructure, token, cloud, or liability cost can break the project.
