# Jyotish Insurance, Claim, Policy, And Coverage Specificity Pack

Use this pack only when the user asks about insurance, policy coverage, claim approval, claim denial, appeal, premium, deductible, coverage limit, beneficiary, insurer, broker, adjuster, indemnity, payout, lapse, renewal, rider, exclusion, pre-existing condition, medical insurance, vehicle insurance, property insurance, business insurance, or whether to buy coverage, renew, cancel, file a claim, appeal, or wait.

Do not treat insurance as ordinary debt, tax, property purchase, health treatment, lawsuit, or generic document approval. If the question is only illness/treatment/surgery, use the health pack. If it asks whether the policy pays, whether the claim is accepted, whether coverage is enough, whether to appeal a denial, or whether to buy/renew/cancel coverage, this pack must be included.

Insurance/Claim Judgment

Jyotish insurance judgment must join Lagna/Moon, 2nd/11th, 8th, 4th, 7th, 3rd/Mercury, 6th, 10th/D10, 12th/Rahu-Ketu/Mars-Saturn, dasha, and gochara. Insurance is not simple wealth; it is a contract that pays only if a loss, policy promise, evidence, and timing all align.

- Lagna/Moon or no-time substitute: insured-risk/exposure capacity, risk discipline, and ability to follow claim procedure.
- 2nd/11th: premium affordability, deductible, reimbursement cash-in, payout usefulness, coverage limit, and gained compensation.
- 8th: insurance payout, indemnity, death/beneficiary money, hidden liability, sudden loss, shared risk, and settlement pressure.
- 4th: insured home, vehicle, land, equipment, fixed asset, stored reserve, and asset condition.
- 7th: insurer/counterparty, beneficiary, named insured/co-insured, contract promise, settlement negotiation, and dispute with insurer.
- 3rd/Mercury: application, policy schedule, claim form, medical/repair report, evidence file, message record, deadline, and appeal wording.
- 6th: claim processing, dispute/objection, adjuster/vendor service, premium administration, illness/accident lane when relevant.
- 10th/D10: insurer approval authority, underwriting standard, employer group plan, formal decision, and public/regulatory escalation.
- 12th/Rahu-Ketu/Mars-Saturn: exclusion, lapse, hidden non-covered loss, penalty, denied claim, fraud suspicion, hospital/loss expense, and preventable drain.
- Dasha/gochara/ashtakavarga when present: claim trigger, payout window, appeal period, renewal/lapse timing, and whether action should be immediate or delayed.

Answer Contract

For insurance/claim questions, the answer must include:

1. one Lagna/Moon or no-time substitute insured-risk/exposure-capacity line;
2. one 2nd/11th premium/deductible/coverage-limit/payout-cash line;
3. one 8th indemnity/insurance-payout/shared-risk/settlement line;
4. one 4th insured-asset/reserve/asset-condition line when asset/property/vehicle/business coverage is involved, or explicitly say why it is not the main axis;
5. one 7th insurer-contract/beneficiary/counterparty-promise line;
6. one 3rd/Mercury claim-file/policy-wording/evidence/deadline line;
7. one 6th broker/adjuster/service-follow-up/claim-dispute line;
8. one 10th/D10 insurer-approval/underwriting/regulator line;
9. one 12th/Rahu-Ketu/Mars-Saturn exclusion/lapse/denial/fraud-risk/uncovered-loss line;
10. one dasha/gochara timing/action line with a clear action: file claim, appeal, renew, buy coverage, increase coverage, reduce rider, cancel after replacement, wait, avoid uncovered risk, prepare evidence, or escalate to regulator.

Do not promise guaranteed payout. If 8th is supportive but 3rd/Mercury or 12th is weak, state "claim possible only with evidence and exclusion control" instead of giving a blanket approval.
