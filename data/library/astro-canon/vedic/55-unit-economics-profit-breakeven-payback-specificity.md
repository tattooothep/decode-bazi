# Jyotish Unit Economics, Profit-Margin, Break-Even, And Payback Specificity Pack

Use this pack only when the user asks about profit quality, margin, unit economics, break-even, payback, LTV/CAC, contribution margin, gross/net profit, cost per order, cost per customer, ARPU/AOV, margin floor, or whether a business model is economically worth doing.

Do not treat unit economics as the same thing as cashflow/runway, price strategy, windfall/speculation, marketing campaign optimization, retention/churn rescue, sales-team QA, team compensation, partner-risk, or technology build. If the user asks "cash left / reserve / runway / burn rate", use the cashflow pack. If the user asks "raise price / discount / coupon / upsell", use the pricing pack. If the user asks "CAC/ROAS for ad creative or funnel traffic", use the marketing pack. This pack is for whether each sale, cohort, customer, product, or plan is profitable after real costs.

## Canon Routing

Jyotish profit judgment must not stop at the 2nd or 11th. Judge the native's profit model by Lagna/Moon, 2nd/11th, 10th/D10, 4th, 3rd/Mercury/7th, 6th, 8th/12th/Rahu-Ketu/Mars-Saturn, then weigh dignity, functional role, varga, bala, dasha, and gochara.

- Lagna/Moon: whether the owner can enforce margin discipline and avoid vanity revenue.
- 2nd/11th: revenue per sale, retained money, collection quality, profit inflow, and real margin.
- 10th/D10: business model, authority, delivery machine, monetization structure, and profit engine.
- 4th: fixed-cost base, retained assets, inventory/office/platform base, and break-even absorption.
- 3rd/Mercury/7th: sales terms, invoice clarity, buyer promise, customer agreement, channel contract, and pricing communication.
- 6th: labor, service burden, contractors, tools, AI, SaaS, support, fulfillment, debt service, and variable cost per unit.
- 8th/12th/Rahu-Ketu/Mars-Saturn: debt, tax, refund drag, hidden labor, bad debt, leakage, rework, disputes, and margin killers.
- Dasha/gocara/ashtakavarga only when packet-supplied: timing for tightening, pausing, testing, or scaling.

## Answer Contract

For unit economics questions, answer with:

1. one Lagna/Moon margin-discipline line;
2. one 2nd/11th revenue-per-unit and collection-quality line;
3. one 10th/D10 profit-model or operating-engine line;
4. one 4th fixed-cost/break-even-base line;
5. one 3rd/Mercury/7th price-term/invoice/channel-promise line;
6. one 6th variable-cost/staff/tool/vendor line;
7. one 8th/12th/Rahu-Ketu/Mars-Saturn hidden-cost/refund/tax/rework-risk line;
8. one timing line from dasha/gocara/ashtakavarga when available;
9. one practical verdict: raise margin floor, stop an unprofitable SKU, repackage, cap variable cost, collect before delivery, or test smaller.

Do not translate strong 11th-house gain into profit unless the 6th, 8th, and 12th are controlled. If dasha supports growth but cost houses are strained, advise proof-of-margin before scale.
