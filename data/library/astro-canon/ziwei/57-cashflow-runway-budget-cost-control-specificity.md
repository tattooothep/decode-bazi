## Core Chain

1. **命宮 / 身宮**: owner discipline, stress tolerance, and ability to enforce budget cuts without panicking.
2. **財帛**: liquid cash, monthly receipts, billing reliability, payment collection, and whether revenue turns into usable money.
3. **田宅**: reserve base, fixed commitments, office/property/infrastructure/subscription base, and whether value is retained.
4. **官祿**: operating model, management authority, internal controls, and whether the business can enforce cost discipline.
5. **兄弟 / 父母**: invoices, terms, documents, SOP, accounting, reports, and proof needed to collect or cut.
6. **夫妻**: customer, vendor, platform, counterparty, payment terms, receivables, and delayed payment.
7. **僕役**: staff, contractors, tools, agencies, platforms, AI/SaaS cost, and operations burn.
8. **疾厄 / 化忌 / 煞曜**: hidden drains, refund/chargeback, tax/debt, emergency cost, failed tools, disputes, and stress leakage.
9. **福德**: comfort spending, morale, anxiety, and whether the owner overspends or overcuts.
10. **Timing**: 大限/流年/流月/流日 and 四化 only when supplied.

## Runway Rule

Runway requires both cash and retention:

- 財帛 = money received,
- 田宅 = money/value retained,
- 官祿 = operating discipline,
- 兄弟/父母 = accounting/reporting/collection documents,
- 夫妻 = customer/vendor payment behavior,
- 僕役 = staff/tool/platform burn,
- 疾厄/化忌/煞曜 = leak, refund, debt, tax, emergency, or hidden liability.

If 財帛 is supported but 田宅 is weak, revenue arrives but reserve does not build. If 田宅 is supported but 財帛 is weak, preserve base and tighten collection. If 僕役 and 疾厄 are pressured, team/tool/operations burn must be capped before expansion.

## Cost-Cut Rule

Name the cost lane instead of saying "save money" broadly:

- **team/tool burn**: 僕役 pressure,
- **customer/vendor/payment delay**: 夫妻 pressure,
- **invoice/report/document confusion**: 兄弟/父母 pressure,
- **fixed commitments/subscription base**: 田宅 pressure,
- **refund/tax/debt/emergency**: 疾厄/化忌/煞曜 pressure,
- **comfort/anxiety spending**: 福德 pressure.

Good cash evidence allows controlled reinvestment. Strong leak evidence requires freeze, cap, collect, renegotiate, or reserve.
