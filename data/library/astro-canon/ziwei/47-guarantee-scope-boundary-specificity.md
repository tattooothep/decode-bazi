## Purpose

Use this pack when the user asks about guarantee, refund-proof offer, money-back guarantee, no-refund policy, scope of work, deliverables, revision limit, unlimited revisions/support, promise boundary, result guarantee, contract boundary, qualification gate, cancellation terms, or how to prevent complaints/refunds before selling.

Do not treat guarantee/scope fit as the same thing as retention after a refund request, exact pricing, delivery model, customer support, or product category. This pack decides what promise is safe to make, what must be excluded, how strict the scope should be, and whether a guarantee should be strong, conditional, narrow, or avoided.

## Guarantee And Scope Chain

1. **命宮 / 身宮**: whether the person can carry a firm promise, strict standard, high-touch responsibility, or result-based guarantee without identity/energy strain.
2. **官祿**: formal authority, professional responsibility, result ownership, public promise, B2B/enterprise accountability, and whether a guarantee improves trust or creates liability.
3. **父母 / 兄弟**: contract wording, policy page, proof, instructions, onboarding, acceptance criteria, revision rules, and whether buyers will understand the boundary.
4. **夫妻**: direct buyer agreement, objections, qualification, refusal/complaint risk, deposit/contract terms, and whether the client can be safely accepted.
5. **僕役**: team/platform/community support burden, reviewer pressure, affiliate/channel obligations, and whether service boundaries can be enforced at scale.
6. **財帛 / 田宅**: refund reserve, retained assets, backend library/data, cost of honoring promises, and whether the offer can absorb guarantee risk.
7. **疾厄 / 化忌 / 煞曜**: hidden labor, burnout, legal/health/financial risk, abusive customer pattern, chargeback/refund stress, and guarantee clauses to avoid.
8. **福德 / 遷移 / 父母**: satisfaction, perceived fairness, prestige/trust, external reputation, emotional comfort, and whether a stricter policy will feel fair or harsh.
9. **大限 / 流年 / 流月 / 流日 / 四化**: when to tighten scope, publish policy, require qualification, test guarantee, remove unlimited support, or pause risky promises.

## Boundary Mapping

Map from packet evidence, not generic star keywords:

- **官祿 + 父母/兄弟 strong, 疾厄 controlled**: clear professional guarantee, written scope, measurable deliverables, acceptance criteria.
- **夫妻 strong, 財帛/田宅 stable**: conditional guarantee with qualification, deposit, milestones, and refund reserve.
- **兄弟/父母 weak or afflicted**: simplify wording; do not add layered guarantees until copy/onboarding/proof is clear.
- **僕役 strong but 疾厄/化忌 active**: scalable policy needed; no unlimited custom support, no open-ended revisions.
- **財帛 weak with 化忌/煞曜 active**: avoid money-back guarantee; use satisfaction check, credit, rework cap, or narrow replacement.
- **福德/遷移 strong**: fairness/reputation can carry a firm no-refund policy if expectations are clean.
- **夫妻/父母 afflicted**: require signed agreement, qualification, proof archive, and dispute path before accepting high-risk buyers.

## Verdict Rules

- If authority and documentation are strong, use a narrow written guarantee rather than vague reassurance.
- If buyer trust is strong but hidden labor is high, use conditional guarantee plus strict scope and revision cap.
- If 化忌/煞曜 concentrate on money, customer, document, or health/stress lanes, do not promise outcomes; guarantee process or deliverables only.
- If communication is weak, fix copy, onboarding, and acceptance criteria before launching a guarantee.
- If support burden is heavy, remove unlimited revisions/support and state out-of-scope items explicitly.
