## Purpose

Use this pack when the user asks about case study, testimonial, review request, customer story, proof library, proof page, result screenshots, before-after, social proof, success story, outcome gallery, client proof, credibility assets, founder story, audit result, customer evidence, or which proof should be shown to make an offer believable.

Do not treat proof/case-study fit as the same thing as sales copy, marketing traffic, reputation crisis, customer persona, onboarding qualification, guarantee boundary, or delivery model. This pack decides what proof type should be collected, which story angle is believable, which result must be documented, and which claim should be removed because the chart cannot carry it.

## Proof Chain

1. **命宮 / 身宮**: whether the owner can stand behind a public claim, be visible, and carry accountability without exaggerating.
2. **官祿 / 父母**: authority position, public face, professional standard, status proof, document proof, public trust, and whether proof should lead with credentials or outcomes.
3. **兄弟**: wording of testimonials, interview questions, review prompts, captions, screenshots, narrative sequence, and whether the evidence is easy to understand.
4. **夫妻**: direct client voice, buyer experience, cooperation, objections solved, contract relationship, and which client stories are safe to publish.
5. **僕役**: community proof, group outcomes, comments, platform reviews, referral evidence, staff-collected proof, and whether proof can scale beyond one hero story.
6. **財帛 / 田宅**: measurable result, money saved/earned, retained asset, stable outcome, implementation proof, receipts, dashboards, and proof that survives scrutiny.
7. **福德**: trust, emotional relief, belief shift, founder meaning, values, and whether the story feels human rather than overproduced.
8. **疾厄 / 化忌 / 煞曜**: overclaim, privacy risk, refund bait, cherry-picking, unverifiable screenshots, hostile clients, medical/legal/financial claim risk, and proof that should be anonymized.
9. **大限 / 流年 / 流月 / 流日 / 四化**: when to collect proof, request reviews, publish case studies, clean old claims, or pause public proof.

## Proof Mapping

Map from packet evidence, not generic star keywords:

- **官祿/父母 strong**: lead with expert authority, method, standard, document proof, public result, or professional before-after.
- **兄弟 strong**: use written testimonials, screenshot sequences, Q&A case study, captions, FAQ proof, or objection-by-objection proof.
- **夫妻 strong**: use direct client quotes, client journey, buyer transformation, objection solved, and consented story.
- **僕役 strong**: use review wall, community outcomes, comment proof, referral proof, group result board, or recurring social proof collection.
- **財帛/田宅 strong**: use quantified proof, retained asset, revenue/cost proof, implementation asset, dashboard, or concrete deliverable evidence.
- **福德 strong**: use founder story, belief shift, relief story, emotional before-after, or values-based proof.
- **疾厄/化忌/煞曜 heavy**: remove exaggerated claims, anonymize sensitive proof, publish caveats, collect stronger evidence first, or avoid using the case.

## Verdict Rules

- If authority is strong but proof assets are weak, publish method/standard first and collect client proof before making result claims.
- If client voice is strong but risk lane is heavy, use anonymized testimonial with consent and avoid numeric promises.
- If money/asset lane is strong, make proof concrete with numbers, documents, before-after assets, or implementation receipts.
- If communication lane is weak, use a structured interview template instead of letting clients write vague praise.
- If hidden-risk lane repeats, do not publish the claim; collect evidence, narrow the wording, or move the proof behind a diagnostic call.
