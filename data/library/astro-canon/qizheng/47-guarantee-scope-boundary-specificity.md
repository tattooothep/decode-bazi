## Purpose

Use this pack when the user asks about guarantee, refund-proof offer, money-back guarantee, no-refund policy, scope of work, deliverables, revision limit, unlimited revisions/support, promise boundary, result guarantee, contract boundary, qualification gate, cancellation terms, or how to prevent complaints/refunds before selling.

This pack decides what promise is safe to make, what must be excluded, how strict the scope should be, and whether a guarantee should be strong, conditional, narrow, or avoided.

## Guarantee And Scope Chain

1. **Three lords**: 命主, 命度/度主, 身宮/身主 show whether the native can personally carry a strong promise, strict standard, or result-based guarantee.
2. **官祿**: formal authority, professional responsibility, result ownership, public promise, B2B/enterprise accountability, and whether a guarantee improves trust or creates liability.
3. **父母 / 兄弟**: contract wording, policy page, proof, instructions, onboarding, acceptance criteria, revision rules, and whether buyers will understand the boundary.
4. **妻妾**: direct buyer agreement, objections, qualification, refusal/complaint risk, deposit/contract terms, and whether the client can be safely accepted.
5. **奴僕**: team/platform/community support burden, reviewer pressure, affiliate/channel obligations, and whether service boundaries can be enforced at scale.
6. **財帛 / 田宅**: refund reserve, retained assets, backend library/data, cost of honoring promises, and whether the offer can absorb guarantee risk.
7. **疾厄 / 仇難**: hidden labor, burnout, legal/health/financial risk, abusive customer pattern, chargeback/refund stress, and guarantee clauses to avoid.
8. **福德 / 相貌**: satisfaction, perceived fairness, prestige/trust, emotional comfort, and whether a stricter policy will feel fair or harsh.
9. **行限 / 限度主 / 木土**: when to tighten scope, publish policy, require qualification, test guarantee, remove unlimited support, or pause risky promises.

## Boundary Mapping

Map from packet evidence, not generic star keywords:

- **官祿 + 父母/兄弟 strong, 疾厄 controlled**: clear professional guarantee, written scope, measurable deliverables, acceptance criteria.
- **妻妾 strong, 財帛/田宅 stable**: conditional guarantee with qualification, deposit, milestones, and refund reserve.
- **兄弟 weak or afflicted**: simplify wording; do not add layered guarantees until copy/onboarding/proof is clear.
- **奴僕 strong but 疾厄 active**: scalable policy needed; no unlimited custom support, no open-ended revisions.
- **財帛 weak with 仇難/疾厄 active**: avoid money-back guarantee; use satisfaction check, credit, rework cap, or narrow replacement.
- **福德/相貌 strong**: fairness/trust can carry a firm no-refund policy if expectations are clean.
- **妻妾/父母 afflicted**: require signed agreement, qualification, proof archive, and dispute path before accepting high-risk buyers.

## Verdict Rules

- If authority and documentation are strong, use a narrow written guarantee rather than vague reassurance.
- If buyer trust is strong but hidden labor is high, use conditional guarantee plus strict scope and revision cap.
- If refund/debt/risk houses repeat, do not promise outcomes; guarantee process or deliverables only.
- If communication is weak, fix copy, onboarding, and acceptance criteria before launching a guarantee.
- If support burden is heavy, remove unlimited revisions/support and state out-of-scope items explicitly.
