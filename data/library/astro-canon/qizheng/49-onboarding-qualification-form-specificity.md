> Runtime derived summary for Fusion Qizheng. It condenses public-domain and licensed-internal operational rules into usable rules only. Do not quote, cite, reproduce, expose OCR/source text, page order, or private paths.

## Purpose

Use this pack when the user asks about onboarding flow, qualification form, intake form, application form, client screening, buyer screening, fit score, application quiz, discovery questionnaire, required documents, pre-call form, red flags, reject criteria, deposit gate, acceptance criteria before service, handoff, setup checklist, kickoff, or how to qualify clients before selling or delivering.

Do not treat onboarding/qualification as the same thing as sales copy, marketing traffic, customer persona, support ticket handling, retention after churn, guarantee boundary, or delivery model. This pack decides what information must be collected, who should be accepted or rejected, where to place the gate, and which risk must be caught before money or work begins.

## Qualification Chain

1. **三主 / 命度 / 度主 / 身主**: whether the seller can enforce boundaries, say no, demand evidence, and carry a gate without people-pleasing.
2. **兄弟**: application questions, intake form wording, written instructions, pre-call questionnaire, FAQ, confirmation messages, and whether clients understand what to submit.
3. **官祿 / 父母**: formal standards, authority, eligibility rules, documents, approval criteria, compliance, contract readiness, and whether the gate looks professional.
4. **妻妾**: direct client fit, buyer attitude, objection pattern, cooperation, payment readiness, and red flags in the client relationship.
5. **奴僕**: group/community/applicant pool, team handoff, staff review, platform workflow, and whether screening can scale without manual overload.
6. **財帛 / 田宅**: deposit, retained value, setup cost, data/assets required, refund exposure, and whether the client has enough commitment before work starts.
7. **福德**: comfort, trust, emotional readiness, expectation alignment, and whether the form calms or scares the right people.
8. **疾厄 / 仇難**: hidden workload, abusive clients, missing data, legal/health/financial risk, scope creep, late payment, chargeback risk, and reject rules.
9. **行限 / 流年 trigger**: when to tighten intake, add deposit, reject more clients, simplify form, automate screening, or pause applications.

## Gate Mapping

Map from packet evidence, not generic star keywords:

- **兄弟 + 官祿/父母 strong**: use a structured application form with required documents, clear eligibility, and written acceptance criteria.
- **妻妾 strong but 疾厄 active**: qualify buyer attitude and scope before call; ask direct red-flag questions.
- **奴僕 strong**: use scalable quiz, score bands, staff review, or automated routing before human calls.
- **財帛/田宅 strong**: use deposit gate, proof of budget, setup checklist, asset upload, and milestone acceptance.
- **福德 strong**: make onboarding reassuring; explain why the questions protect both sides.
- **兄弟 weak**: shorten the form; use multiple-choice, examples, and required uploads instead of vague free text.
- **疾厄/仇難 heavy**: reject vague, rushed, hostile, unpaid, or missing-data clients; no manual rescue without paid scope.

## Verdict Rules

- If client lane is strong but hidden-labor risk is high, accept selectively with deposit and document gate.
- If copy/form lane is weak, simplify intake before adding more questions.
- If authority/document lane is strong, publish eligibility rules and make the gate explicit.
- If group/applicant lane is strong, use form + scoring + assistant review; do not manually interview everyone.
- If risk lane repeats, reject before payment or require paid diagnostic first.
