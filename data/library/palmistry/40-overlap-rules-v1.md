# Hourkey Palm Overlap Rules v1.0

Overlap rules are the strongest part of Hourkey Palm Canon because they identify patterns that multiple civilizations read in the same general direction.

## HK.PALM.V1.OVL.01 - Three Main Axes

- tier: T1
- source_id: `HK.PALM.V1.T1.01`
- evidence: `CN.LINE.3CORE`, `IN.LINE.3FIRST`, `WE.LINE.MAJOR4`
- shared meaning: read major structure before small signs.
- use: establish confidence and coverage.
- guard: meanings are analogous, not identical.

## HK.PALM.V1.OVL.02 - Line Quality

- tier: T1
- source_id: `HK.PALM.V1.T1.02`, `HK.PALM.V1.T1.03`
- evidence: Chinese line depth, Indian deep/glossy lines, Western line quality.
- shared meaning: clear/deep/ordered means stable expression; shallow/rough/confused means diffusion.
- use: all topics.
- guard: no class/status language.

## HK.PALM.V1.OVL.03 - Breaks/Cuts

- tier: T1
- source_id: `HK.PALM.V1.T1.04`
- evidence: Chinese broken/scattered line, Indian cut line, Western broken/crossed line.
- shared meaning: interruption, pressure, redirection.
- use: turning points and topic-specific caution.
- guard: no death/illness/disaster.

## HK.PALM.V1.OVL.04 - Finger Proportion

- tier: T1
- source_id: `HK.PALM.V1.T1.05`
- evidence: Chinese long/slender fingers, Indian long/lean fingers, Western finger proportion.
- shared meaning: long/slender tends analytic/refined; short/thick tends practical/direct.
- guard: no value hierarchy.

## HK.PALM.V1.OVL.05 - Palm Texture

- tier: T1
- source_id: `HK.PALM.V1.T1.06`
- evidence: Chinese soft/fine vs rough/hard, Indian smooth/rough, Western fine/coarse/firm.
- shared meaning: texture shows how energy is carried.
- guard: no health diagnosis or moral judgment.

## HK.PALM.V1.OVL.06 - Marks Require Clarity

- tier: T1
- source_id: `HK.PALM.V1.T1.07`
- evidence: all three systems use marks/signs.
- shared meaning: special signs can modify interpretation only if visible.
- guard: no invented marks.

## HK.PALM.V1.OVL.07 - Bright/Red Palm

- tier: T2
- source_id: `HK.PALM.V1.T2.01`
- evidence: Chinese qise, Indian red/lac-like palm tone.

- guard: lighting first, no medical claim.

- tier: T2
- source_id: `HK.PALM.V1.T2.02`

## HK.PALM.V1.OVL.09 - Triangle

## HK.PALM.V1.OVL.10 - Upward Line

- tier: T2
- source_id: `HK.PALM.V1.T2.04`
- evidence: Indian upward line, Western upward fate/Jupiter branch.
- shared meaning: aspiration and effort to rise.
- guard: no caste/fixed career claim.

## HK.PALM.V1.OVL.11 - Line Density

- tier: T2
- source_id: `HK.PALM.V1.T2.05`
- evidence: Chinese/Indian warnings about excessive or absent lines; Western sensitivity reading for many fine lines.
- shared meaning: many lines can show mental load/sensitivity; few lines can show simplicity/directness.
- guard: check image clarity first.

## HK.PALM.V1.OVL.MESO.01 - Mesopotamian Body-Line Historical Support

- tier: history-support
- source_id: `HK.PALM.V1.OVL.MESO.01`
- linked_rule: `HK.PALM.V1.MESO.02`
- evidence: Mesopotamian physiognomic body-line/hand-line material compared with Indian physiognomy.
- shared meaning: visible lines on the body/hand can be treated as symbolic sign material.
- use: source explanation, G turning points, and support for reading visible lines only.
- guard: does not upgrade to T1, does not define Western named lines, no exact timing.

## HK.PALM.V1.OVL.MESO.02 - Located Marks Across Traditions

- tier: support-only
- source_id: `HK.PALM.V1.OVL.MESO.02`
- linked_rule: `HK.PALM.V1.MESO.04`
- evidence: Mesopotamian marks/blemishes as located signs; Chinese/Indian/Western special marks.
- shared meaning: a visible mark modifies the line or region where it appears.
- use: C/D/F/G only when the mark is clear and already covered by a Chinese/Indian/Western rule.
- guard: no medical diagnosis, no disaster, no invented marks.

## Overlap Priority

Use in this order:

1. T1 main structure and line quality.
2. T1 breaks/cuts and texture/fingers.
3. T2 color/fish/triangle/upward lines/density only if visible.
4. T3 school-specific material as supporting detail.
5. Mesopotamian support only as historical/context evidence after the palm feature is already supported by T1/T2/T3.
